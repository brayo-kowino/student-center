Try closing and reopening the app. If it continues to freeze, clear the app's cache or reinstall it. You can also restart your phone to resolve minor glitches. Please note that if the app is not responding, crashing, or freezing frequently, this could indicate missing permissions or insufficient resources, which may severely affect the app's functionality and potentially cause it to stop working entirely.

Make sure notifications are enabled in both the app and your device settings. If you're still missing notifications, check if the app has the required permissions to access your notifications.

Since the app is not available on the Play Store, updates are sent directly through the app UI. When an update is available, you will be prompted to download and install it through the app. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the update.

Frequent crashes or freezing can be caused by insufficient storage, missing permissions, or an outdated app version. Make sure your device has enough storage, the app has the necessary permissions, and that you are using the latest app version.

If the media player or video player is crashing or showing limited files, it may be due to insufficient permissions or a bug in the app. Ensure that your app is updated to the latest version and that the necessary permissions are granted. If the issue persists, you can try restarting the app or reinstalling it. Please note that the application is still in an active development phase, and some bugs may occur. These bugs will be addressed through continuous updates.

If media files are not showing up and the media player is not responding as expected, you can try manually rescanning your device's storage. To do this, go to the app's media player screen and select "Rescan Storage." from a menu accessible when a three dot icon is clicked. The icons are on the bottom right next to "Now Playing." If the issue persists after rescanning, you may need to contact support for further assistance.
